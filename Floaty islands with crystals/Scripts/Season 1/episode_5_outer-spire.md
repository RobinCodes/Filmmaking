## Exposition

### New exposition this episode

> This episode picks up directly after Episode 4. Vey has sent sealed summons to both the Crystal Warden and the Chancellor, and the Crystal Warden has responded asking for "around three days" more to finish her degradation surveys at the outer cluster sites (see Ep4, Scene 13).

> Maren is currently on the outer cluster survey Vey sent her on, which has brought her to the rebuilt outpost from Episode 3, checking its one remaining crystal as part of that same survey.

> Doran is unseen again this episode. What we know so far: vague, evasive excuses relayed through his office (Ep4), and now, a growing suspicion that his absence predates the current crisis by much longer than he's claimed.

## Returning Characters in this episode:

*Voicing notes carried over from previous episodes for reference.*

### Thread A - Tethermoor / Wren & June

- **Wren** - protagonist. **Female.** Late teens. The cluster's engineer/fixer — the one who keeps things running when no one else can. Practical, a little stubborn, takes responsibility for things that aren't her fault. Voice should be grounded and direct, she doesn't waste words, doesn't perform emotion, but it leaks through at the edges. Not loud. Most comfortable when she's solving something with her hands.

- **June** - Wren's best friend; airship mechanic and occasional pilot. **Female.**  Confident, a little reckless, talks fast and teases easily, but genuinely reliable when it counts. The contrast to Wren: where Wren is careful and methodical, June is impulsive and improvises. Voice should be loose, energetic, quick with a joke, but capable of being completely serious in a heartbeat when something actually matters.

### Thread C - The Traders

- **Rell** - leader of the trading outfit. **Female.** Mid forties. Morally grey, resourceful, has history with the Spire she doesn't explain. Speaks with a light pirate flavor - "ahoy," "aye," -  without playing it as a full pirate caricature, more like an old habit of speech from wherever she's from. Voice should be smooth, unhurried, conversational even mid-con. She rarely raises her voice, can talk a lot but knows when to listen, which makes people fill silences by oversharing. Knows a lot of information.

- **Hask** - Rell's second-in-command. **Male.** Loyal to Rell specifically, not to the work. Skeptical of outsiders, slow to warm up. Voice should be blunt, economical, dry humor delivered flat. Doesn't smile much, isn't humorless. 

### Thread D - Comic relief / Bram

- **Bram** - **Male.** Older, weathered, has owned and lost more airships than he can remember, and likes to talk about them. Not actually sad, performs sadness for sympathy and free drinks. Voice should be loud, theatrical, given to wild and escalating exaggeration. Comic timing comes from overexaggerating, and making up obviously fake stories. Kind of clumsy at times.

### Thread B - The Spire

- **Overseer Castellan Vey** — head of the ruling Council. **Female.** Composed, tired in a way, she hides well. Voice should be measured and quiet even under pressure — she rarely raises her voice, which makes her authority feel earned rather than forced.

- **Councilor Commander Brask** - head of law enforcement and the military. **Male.** Clipped, impatient, treats hesitation as weakness.

- **Councilor Treasurer Ottlin** - head of finance. **Male.** Cautious, non-committal, the kind of person who waits to see which way the wind blows before speaking, though as Treasurer, that caution is at least partly professional, not just temperament. Voice should be soft, fond of phrases like "in principle" and "that said." Not unintelligent, just conflict-averse. Ottlin avoids conflict to protect himself, but he genuinely does need the numbers before he'll move.

- **Reska** - a young records clerk, junior, works near Vey's office. **Female.** Quiet, observant, professionally invisible in the way junior staff often are. Doesn't speak much, but notices everything. Voice should be small, careful, deferential, she's good at her job specifically because she doesn't make herself noticed.

## New Characters this episode:

- **Councilor Crystal Warden Maren** - holds the Crystal Warden seat on the Council. **Female, mid-to-late forties**, roughly Rell's age. Field-first, impatient with Council politics, more comfortable with a crystal in her hands than a gavel. Voice should be brisk, plainspoken, no patience for nonsense, but not unkind (she talks to Bram like he's a minor inconvenience, not an idiot.)
- **Councilor Chancellor Doran** - holds the Chancellor seat on the Council. **Male, approximately 60.** (not actually seen, just wrote him here so I don't forget)
- **Clerk Marrow** - the Outer Spire rope-wall checkpoint official. **Male.** Not military, not suspicious of contraband so much as obsessed with paperwork and quotas. Dry, procedural, entirely unbothered by charm because he genuinely doesn't care who anyone is, only whether the form is filled out correctly.
- **Caravan Master Fenn** - one-off, functional. Runs a supply caravan headed for the outer trade routes; Bram's mark.
- **Courier** - same functional role as Ep4, dispatched with sealed messages.

### Unnamed / one-time characters appearing in this episode:

- **Market vendor(s)** - one-off, functional role, 1-2 background voices.
- **Outpost worker** - one-off, functional role, background repair work at the Ep3 outpost.
- **Bystander** - one-off, functional role, listening to Bram

## Scene 1 - Open sky, approaching the rope wall
*(Thread A & C. ~20 seconds.)*

**SETTING:** Both ships flying in formation, same as Ep2 & Ep3. Ahead, the rope wall: cables strung between anchor towers, platforms, a queue of ships traveling toward it one at a time.

*(Wren stands at the rail, looking ahead. The scale of it should land on her, bigger than she expected, the way the Spire itself did back in Ep2.)*

> **JUNE:** *(joining her, low whistle)* That's a lot of rope for one wall.

> **WREN:** *(quiet)* Didn't think it'd be a wall. Thought it'd be more like... the patrol stop. One ship, a couple of officers.

> **JUNE:** Guess the Spire doesn't do "a couple" of anything.

*(On Rell's ship, alongside, Rell is already businesslike, checking papers, not looking at the view at all. Hask notices Wren staring and gives her a small nod, like: "yeah, it's a lot.")*

> **RELL:** *(calling over, not looking up from her papers)* Get used to queues, loves. The Spire runs on 'em.

## Scene 2 - The queue
*(Thread A & C. ~20 seconds.)*

**SETTING:** Ships going forward, one at a time. Wren and June's ship sits behind Rell and Hask's in line.

> **JUNE:** *(watching the line barely move)* How long do you think this takes?

> **HASK:** *(from the other ship, not even glancing over)* Depends how many idiots ahead of us filled out their forms wrong.

> **JUNE:** *(muttering)* Great. Comforting.

*(Wren's looking past the queue, toward the wall itself, still wondering about something.)*

## Scene 3 - Bram
*(Thread D. ~25 seconds.)*

**SETTING:** Tethermoor, the bar from ep2&ep3, or the town square. Bram is mid-pitch to someone.

> **BRAM:** *(loud, wounded dignity)* - and that's how I lost the last of it, see, straight off the side of the island, and if that's not a story worth a drink and a little traveling coin, I don't know what is!

*(A pause. No response. Bystander walks off.)*

> **BRAM:** It's a TRUE story!

*(He sits on a stool, alone now, muttering to himself.)*

> **BRAM:** *(to no one)* Nobody appreciates a tragedy anymore.

*(A ship arrives at the dock, Bram looks at it)*

> **BRAM:** A-ha! That's it! 

*(Starts walking towards the ship, cut.)*

## Scene 4 - Wren watches Rell
*(Thread A & C. ~20 seconds.)*

**SETTING:** Still in the queue, ships close enough for conversation across the gap.

*(Wren's gaze drifts to Rell again, a bit too long. June notices it.)*

> **JUNE:** *(quiet, just to Wren)* You're doing the thing again.

> **WREN:** What thing?

> **JUNE:** The "staring until it's weird" thing. You've been doing it since the outpost.

> **WREN:** *(not denying it)* I just want her to say something that isn't half an answer for once.

> **JUNE:** *(gently)* Yeah. Me too, honestly.

## Scene 5 - Hask and Wren, a moment apart
*(Thread A & C. ~40 seconds.)*

**SETTING:** Rell has stepped off to get some papers. Hask looking at Wren from the other ship.

> **HASK:** You've been chewing on something since the outpost.

> **WREN:** *(surprised he brought it up)* Is she lying to me?

> **HASK:** *(considering the question seriously, which is itself telling)* No. *(pause)* She's not lying to you. She's just not telling you everything. Different thing.

> **WREN:** Doesn't feel that different from here.

> **HASK:** *(almost sympathetic)* Fair. *(pause)* For what it's worth, she doesn't even tell *me* everything either. Never has. It's not personal. It's just how she keeps herself standing.

> **WREN:** That supposed to make me feel better?

> **HASK:** *(flat, honest)* No. Just supposed to be true.

*(He walks away as Rell returns, conversation over.)*

## Scene 6 - Bram, second scheme
*(Thread D. ~45 seconds.)*

**SETTING:** Tethermoor docks. Bram has clearly moved on to Plan B: approaching **Caravan Master Fenn**, who's loading crates onto a supply caravan headed for the outer trade routes.

> **BRAM:** *(already mid-pitch, gesturing grandly)* - and what this operation *needs*, Fenn, if I may be so bold, is a man who knows the outer routes like the back of his own weathered, well-traveled hand.

> **FENN:** *(not looking up from the crates)* All the ships you've been on were either shot down, confiscated or you crashed them.

> **BRAM:** *(replying instantly, almost cutting him off)* Which is exactly why I'm so eager to learn! Fresh eyes, Fenn. Fresh eyes see things old ones miss.

> **FENN:** *(sighing, clearly has heard a hundred versions of this pitch)* I don't need eyes. I need someone who won't fall off the caravan.

> **BRAM:** *(hand on heart)* I have never once fallen off anything I wasn't specifically pushed from.

*(Fenn stares at him for a long second. Bram stares back, undeterred, radiating false confidence.)*

> **BRAM:** Well?

> **FENN:** *(finally, exasperated)* Fine. Load crates, don't talk to the animals, and if you fall off I am **NOT** turning the caravan around.

> **BRAM:** *(already climbing aboard, triumphant)* Wouldn't dream of asking!

*(Cut.)*

## Scene 7 - Checkpoint, arrival
*(Thread A & C. ~20 seconds.)*

**SETTING:** The checkpoint station: a long counter, stacks of forms, stamps, a queue of travelers being processed one at a time. **Clerk Marrow** sits behind the counter, unhurried, methodical.

> **MARROW:** *(not looking up, holding out a hand)* Manifests. All ships.

*(Rell hands hers over with easy confidence, clearly expecting this to go the way it usually does.)*

> **RELL:** *(warm, practiced)* Lovely bit of paperwork weather we're having.

> **MARROW:** *(still not looking up)* There's no such thing as paperwork weather.

> **RELL:** *(undeterred, leaning on the counter)* No, but you looked up for a second there, didn't ya?

> **MARROW:** *(flat, finally looking up just to make a point of not being charmed)* I looked up because your ship's registration number is smudged. Not because of the weather comment.

*(Rell's smile doesn't falter, but there's a small flicker, this one isn't going to be easy.)*

## Scene 8 - Checkpoint, the flag 
*(Thread A & C. ~50 seconds. Max on-screen: 4.)*

**SETTING:** Same counter. Marrow has been flipping through a second ledger, cross-referencing something. Continuous with previous scene, add transition like Marrow going for some papers.

> **MARROW:** *(dry)* Your ship's clean. You, however, have a flag.

> **RELL:** *(too casual)* Do I now?

> **MARROW:** *(reading)* Outstanding notation from an unclosed dispute. Filed... *(checking date)* ...recently enough it hasn't cleared review.

*(Hask shoots Rell a look. She doesn't return it.)*

> **RELL:** *(light, deflecting)* Paperwork's paperwork. I'm sure it'll clear on its own.

> **MARROW:** *(unmoved)* It clears when you go to dispute it, or it doesn't clear, and you don't cross the wall today. Or optionally if a Spire-recognized party vouches for you, you can pass, and it will clear in seven days on good behaviour.

*(A pause. Marrow looks past Rell, to Wren and June.)*

> **MARROW:** *(checking their papers)* You two are listed as petitioners. That's a recognized purpose. *(to Wren and June)* Are you traveling with this woman by your own arrangement?

*(Wren and June exchange a glance. This is clearly not a question they expected to matter.)*

> **WREN:** *(carefully)* ...Yes.

> **MARROW:** Then you can vouch for her. Sign here, and her passage is your responsibility until she's processed clear on the other side. Any violation on her part during that window reflects on your standing, not just hers.

*(Silence. Wren looks at June. June looks at Rell, who is, notably, saying nothing at all.)*

## Scene 9 - Checkpoint, the cost
*(Thread A & C. ~25 seconds.)*

**SETTING:** Same counter, moments later. The papers are signed. Marrow stamps everything with total indifference to the weight of what just happened. Continuous from previous scene, they sign the papers.

> **MARROW:** *(already moving on to the next ship in line)* Next.

*(They step away from the counter. Wren looks at the paper in her hand like it might bite her.)*

> **JUNE:** *(quiet)* Did we just... take on her debt?

> **WREN:** *(reading it again)* Not the debt. Just if she does something wrong while we're all in there, it's on us too.

> **HASK:** *(low, to Rell, once they're a few steps clear)* You knew that was still open.

> **RELL:** *(finally, quieter than usual)* I knew it was a possibility.

> **HASK:** That's not the same as knowing.

*(Rell doesn't answer. She looks back at Wren and June for a second, then keeps walking.)*

## Scene 10 - Wren confronts Rell
*(Thread A & C. ~30 seconds.)*

**SETTING:** A quieter stretch past the checkpoint, on the ways back to the processed airships. Wren catches up to Rell. June hangs back a few steps, close enough to hear.

> **WREN:** *(not shouting, but done being patient)* We just signed our names to your problem. I think that earns me an actual answer.

> **RELL:** *(stopping, considering her for a long moment)* ...Fair enough. *(pause, this costs her something)* The outpost. The housing. I knew it was worse than the report said. I'd seen buildings like it fail before. I didn't say anything because saying something would've meant explaining how I knew.

> **WREN:** And how did you know?

> **RELL:** *(gently but firmly)* That's the part I'm not gonna tell you. *(pause, genuinely)* But that much, the housing, that's true. 

*(Wren studies her. It's not enough. But it's not nothing either.)*

> **WREN:** *(quiet)* That's not an answer, Rell. It's half of one.

> **RELL:** *(already walking away, boarding her ship)* It's the half you're getting today.

*(Wren doesn't follow immediately.)*

## Scene 11 - June checks on Wren
*(Thread A & C. ~25 seconds.)*

**SETTING:** On June's ship, a few minutes after the previous scene

> **JUNE:** *(carefully)* You okay?

> **WREN:** *(not really)* I don't know what I want from her anymore. The truth, or just... to stop being surprised by her.

> **JUNE:** *(takes a breath, honest)* For what it's worth, I don't think she's lying about the parts she does tell you. I just think there's a lot she's not telling anyone.

> **JUNE:**  Then it's probably true. Man doesn't waste words on comfort.

## Scene 15 - Bram arrives at the outpost
*(Thread D. ~50 seconds.)*

**SETTING:** The Ep3 outpost, now visibly scarred and partially rebuilt: patched walls, a "closed for repairs" sign on one structure, an outpost worker sweeping debris in the background. Bram disembarks from the caravan, looking around, entirely oblivious.

> **BRAM:** *(taking it in, unbothered)* Rough week for someone.

*(He wanders toward the damaged building. A woman not yet revealed to be Maren (dressed plainly, no obvious sign of rank) is crouched near the remaining crystal housing (blown open by the explosion), running some kind of instrument over it, frowning at the reading.)*

> **BRAM:** *(approaching, friendly, oblivious)* Afternoon! Lovely disaster you've got here. What happened, dragon?

> **MAREN:** *(not looking up, dry)* Structural failure. Overloaded housing.

> **BRAM:** *(nodding, clearly making this up as he goes)* Ah, yes. Classic overloaded housing. Happens to the best of us.

*(Maren finally glances at him, unimpressed, but not unkind.)*

> **MAREN:** Do you work here?

> **BRAM:** *(without hesitation)* Absolutely not. Just arrived. Fresh eyes. *(pause)* Actually, funny thing, feels like I've heard about this exact place. Big bang, wasn't it? Someone said an explosion.

*(Maren's attention sharpens slightly at that.)*

> **MAREN:** *(carefully)* Where'd you hear that?

> **BRAM:** *(vague, already losing interest in his own thread)* Somewhere. Dock talk. You know how it is.

*(Maren looks back at the crystal housing, troubled. She makes a note.)*

> **MAREN:** *(mostly to herself)* That's not in any of the official reports I was given.

> **BRAM:** *(cheerfully, already wandering off toward the caravan again)* Well! Good luck with the dragon situation!

*(Maren watches him go, then turns back to the crystal, running the instrument over it again, more carefully this time. Her expression says this is going to take longer than three days.)*

## Scene 16 - Inner Spire in view

> **WREN:** *(just looking)* Yeah.

## Scene 17 - The Spire, Vey and Brask discuss the deadline
*(Thread B. ~30 seconds.)*

**SETTING:** Somewhere in the Spire, Mothercrystal vault, Vey's office or a corridor, same as prior episodes. Brask enters with a report in hand.

> **BRASK:** *(without greeting, as usual)* Word from the Crystal Warden's courier. She's not three days out anymore.

> **VEY:** *(already bracing)* How many now?

> **BRASK:** *(checking the report)* She's found something at one of the outpost sites. Wants at least four, maybe five more days to run it down properly before she comes back with numbers she can stand behind.

*(Vey closes her eyes for a moment, the same brief allowance of tiredness we've seen before.)*

> **VEY:** *(steady, deciding something)* Then we stop waiting on a number that keeps moving. Council convenes in six days. Warden or no Warden.

> **BRASK:** *(careful)* And the Chancellor?

> **VEY:** *(flat)* Gets the same six days. Only this time, it isn't a request, and it isn't gentle.

## Scene 18 - Vey sends the letter
*(Thread B. ~25 seconds.)*

**SETTING:** Vey's office, shortly after. The Courier from Ep4 waits by the door.

> **VEY:** *(sealing a letter, handing it over)* This goes to the Chancellor directly. Not his office, not his clerks. Him.

> **COURIER:** *(taking it carefully)* And if he's not where his office claims, Overseer?

> **VEY:** *(flat, final)* Then find out why not. Tell him six days. Tell him the Council will convene with or without his seat filled, and an empty chair speaks for itself.

## Scene 19 - Ottlin's office
*(Thread B. ~40 seconds.)*

**SETTING:** Ottlin's office: smaller, tidier, ledgers stacked with more care than Brask's office would ever have. Ottlin at his desk. Brask enters.

> **OTTLIN:** *(without looking up)* If you're here for numbers again, Commander, I still don't have the ones you want.

> **BRASK:** *(sitting without being asked)* I'm not here for numbers. I'm here because the Crystal Warden herself just told the Overseer she found something bad enough to delay her own return.

*(That gets Ottlin's attention. He sets down his pen.)*

> **OTTLIN:** *(careful, the way he always is)* "Something bad" isn't a number either, Commander.

> **BRASK:** *(leaning forward)* No. But it's the Warden saying it, not me. You've been waiting to hear it from someone who isn't me. Now you have.

*(Ottlin doesn't answer right away. It's not agreement, but it's the closest thing to it he's given so far.)*

> **OTTLIN:** *(quietly)* ...In principle, that does change things. *(pause)* Get me her actual findings when she has them. Not before.

> **BRASK:** *(standing, satisfied enough)* That's all I needed today.

## Scene 20 - Reska's discovery
*(Thread B. Closing beat. ~20 seconds.)*

**SETTING:** The records office, same quiet corner as Ep4. Reska is pulling old attendance logs, likely to help prepare something for the Council session. She flips back further than she needs to, and stops.

*(She frowns, flips back another page. Then another. Her expression shifts from routine to genuinely unsettled.)*

> **RESKA:** *(barely audible, to herself)* That's... not months. That's longer than months.

*(She glances toward the door, then back down at the documents: the Chancellor's actual attendance record, sparse entries stretching back far further than his current absence would account for. She doesn't move for a long moment, clearly deciding whether this is hers to bring to anyone.)*
