# Episode 3: "The Rogue"
**Series:** *Floaty Islands with crystals* (working title)
**Runtime target:** 18-24 minutes (measures 12 minutes 30 seconds by approximate)
**Threads featured:**
- A (Tethermoor/Wren & June) - primary main storyline, now entangled with Thread C
- B (The Spire) - secondary storyline with "the government"
- C (The Traders - Rell & Hask) - Traders, secondary storyline supporting the main thread
- D (Bram/Comic relief) - comic relief, now separated from the bar, on the road on Tethermoor (some previously Thread A characters appear in thread D this episode, as it takes place on Tethermoor)

**Max on-screen at once:** 4 (Scene 14)

---

## Exposition
> No new exposition

### Previous exposition related to this episode:

> The Sundering: big event a few hundred years ago, which scattered the world into floating islands with crystals.

> "Clusters" are a small group of islands held up and together by a crystal. Most of them have a Central Island, which is considerably larger than the few smaller ones surrounding it. Examples are Tethermoor and the Spire.

> The Spire refers to "the central cluster", its main island, or the government running on it (the government has power through the distribution of crystals, the world is otherwise really roughly or not at all regulated).

> Crystals are what make islands float/keep them together. Smaller crystals are "grown" on the Mothercrystal, and then distributed by The Spire. Smaller crystals power the airships, but some older ones float via balloon. the crystals only make the ships float, they still need an engine to move.

> The Hallowsen quake (working name, AI) is some kind of crystal shaking event, happened before most of our main cast was born, the only one to really live through it is Yara, as she's really old (I mean 100+ or possibly 200+, whatever fits the story best). Marl was only a few years old back then, but he really likes to talk about experiencing it.

## Returning Characters in this episode:

*Voicing notes carried over from previous episodes for reference.*

### Thread A - Tethermoor / Wren & June

- **Wren** - protagonist. **Female.** Late teens. The cluster's engineer/fixer - the one who keeps things running when no one else can. Practical, a little stubborn, takes responsibility for things that aren't her fault. Voice should be grounded and direct, she doesn't waste words, doesn't perform emotion, but it leaks through at the edges. Not loud. Most comfortable when she's solving something with her hands.

- **June** - Wren's best friend; airship mechanic and occasional pilot. **Female.**  Confident, a little reckless, talks fast and teases easily, but genuinely reliable when it counts. The contrast to Wren: where Wren is careful and methodical, June is impulsive and improvises. Voice should be loose, energetic, quick with a joke, but capable of being completely serious in a heartbeat when something actually matters.

### Thread B - The Spire

- **Overseer Castellan Vey** - head of the ruling Council. **Female.** Composed, tired in a way, she hides well. Voice should be measured and quiet even under pressure - she rarely raises her voice, which makes her authority feel earned rather than forced.

- **Councilor Brask** - hardliner on the Council. **Male.** Mentioned / seen briefly. Sets up future conflict. Voice should be clipped, impatient - someone who treats hesitation as weakness.

### Thread C - The Traders

- **Rell** - leader of the trading outfit. **Female.** Mid-thirties to forties. Morally grey, resourceful, has history with the Spire she doesn't explain. Speaks with a light pirate flavor - "ahoy," "aye," -  without playing it as a full pirate caricature, more like an old habit of speech from wherever she's from. Voice should be smooth, unhurried, conversational even mid-con. She rarely raises her voice and listens more than she talks, which makes people fill silences by oversharing, knows a lot of information.

- **Hask** - Rell's second-in-command. **Male.** Loyal to Rell specifically, not to the work. Skeptical of outsiders, slow to warm up. Voice should be blunt, economical, dry humor delivered flat. Doesn't smile much, isn't humorless. 

### Thread D - Comic relief / Bram

- **Bram** - the sailor who sold June his airship (mentioned, unseen, in Episode 1). **Male.** Older, weathered, has owned and lost more airships than he can remember, and likes to talk about them. Not actually sad, performs sadness for sympathy and free drinks. Voice should be loud, theatrical, given to wild and escalating exaggeration. Comic timing comes from overexaggerating, and making up obviously fake stories. Kind of clumsy at times.

- **Doss** - the cluster's pragmatic leader. **Male.** Not unkind, but conflict-averse; has spent years keeping Tethermoor's head down so the Spire never has a reason to notice them. Voice should be tired-but-steady, a bit gruff, the tone of someone used to being the one who says "no" so nobody else has to. Softens noticeably around Pip.

- **Yara** - the cluster elder. **Female.** Keeper of pre-Sundering stories and fragments. Calm, speaks carefully, knows more than she says. Voice should be slow and deliberate, she never rushes a sentence, which makes it land harder when she finally says something blunt. Warm, but a little distant, like she's always thinking two steps ahead.

### Unnamed / one time characters appearing in this episode:

- **Clerk 1** (registration desk) - one-off, functional role.
- **Clerk 2** (upper management) - one-off, functional role.
- **Director Pell** (upper management) - one-off, functional role.

## Scene 1 - Open sky, approaching the outpost
*(Thread A & C. ~30 seconds.)*

**SETTING:** Morning. Two ships flying close together, June's small patched-up ship roped to Rell's larger trader vessel. The outpost is visible ahead in the distance. The outpost consists of only one island, making up the whole cluster. The Island is circular, with a smaller circular island attached to it on the side. The Main part of the island has many extensions as a dock, for ships to park. On the main island is a circular building, with greek style pillars, on the smaller island part is a similar building, the two attached via a corridor. The lower hanging part of the island has rooms hanging out, little offices. It's clear this outpost has way outgrown itself. Not in the best condition.

> **RELL:** *(calling over from her ship)* Aye, that's a proper outpost, that one. Used to be half that size. Spire's been pouring money into it the last few years.

> **WREN:** *(looking at the structure, to herself)* Weird island shape... 

*(notices both of the islands have a hanging down part, signifying that it's two islands with two crystals, rather than one)*

> **WREN:** *(louder, asking)* Two crystals?

> **RELL:** Sharp eyes. Old half runs on the original, new half they bolted on a second when they expanded. *(short pause)* Which works out nicely for us, actually.

> **JUNE:** *(wary)* Why does that sound like the start of the favour?

> **RELL:** *(grinning, not denying it)* Because it is. Bring her in slow, June, we'll talk specifics once we're docked.

*(Cut.)*

## Scene 2 - The dock, briefing
*(Thread A & C. ~75 seconds.)*

**SETTING:** A large dock, not too well maintained, but official; Actual Spire-marked crates, a few uniformed officials moving between buildings. Rell pulls Wren and June aside before they head toward the registration desk, Hask is left behind. 

> **RELL:** *(low, businesslike, but still easy)* Right. Here's how this goes. You two are mechanics, here to repair the secondary crystal housing. The new one, the bolted-on half.

> **WREN:** We don't know anything about this outpost's housing.

> **RELL:** You don't need to, I do. *(pulls out a rope, hands it to Wren)* This goes around the housing's support line, where it's frayed. Outposts like this always have a frayed support line, it's the first thing that goes. And this *(pulls out a small electrical device)* is an energy controller. Wire it into the housing's electrical panel. It'll even out the load.

> **JUNE:** *(taking it, turning it over)* Even out the load. That's it?

> **RELL:** That's it. One thing though, don't mention the electrical system being faulty at check-in. Outpost staff don't love it getting out that their electrical system is held together by hope and prayer. Just say you're here for the rope repair, they'll know electrical's included. Professional courtesy.

> **WREN:** *(taking the rope, looking at it)* This rope's not the quality used for crystal housing. It's the kind we'd use on a garden trellis back home.

> **RELL:** *(easy, already half-turned away)* It's a temporary fix, love. Temporary fixes don't need to be pretty. Especially when they order it cheap. *(over her shoulder)* Meet me outside the main entrance after. I'll be talking to some old friends in upper management.

*(Rell walks off. Wren looks at the rope and the "energy controller" again, frowning slightly. Not enough to act on it, just enough to notice. June is already walking toward the registration desk)*

> **JUNE:** *(not looking back)* You coming, or are you going to keep interrogating a rope?

> **WREN:** *(following, quiet)* ...Coming.

## Scene 5 - Crystal housing
*(Thread A. ~35 seconds.)*

**SETTING:** We see Wren and June enter the before mentioned door, and walk down some stairs. Circular room with a larger crystal inside (2-3 times Tethermoor's) connected to the wall via ropes, and energy pipes coming out of it.

> **JUNE:** *(eyeing the storage building)* What exactly did Rell say they store here?

> **WREN:** *(focused on the housing)* Not our business. We're here for the support line.

> **JUNE:** *(keeping watch, casual)* You really think a garden rope's gonna hold up a building?

> **WREN:** *(tying it off)* No. *(quick pause)* But it'll hold for now, and "for now" seems to be the only standard anyone around here's working with.

## Scene 6 - "Upper management"
*(Thread C. ~70 seconds.)*

**SETTING:** A corridor, then a records office. Papers, documents, shelving, a working space rather than a grand one. Rell approaches a clerk at a desk outside a door marked something official.

> **RELL:** *(warm, easy, already mid-charm)* Ahoy. I'm told... *(hesitates a little bit, but still very easy. Looks at door with name on it)* Director Pell still handles outpost archives personally?

> **CLERK:** *(wary)* He does. Do you have an appointment?

> **RELL:** *(laughs lightly)* No, but I have history. Tell him Rell's here. He'll remember the name, or he'll pretend not to, which is its own kind of answer.

*(The clerk hesitates, then disappears through the door. Returns.)*

> **CLERK:** *(anxious)* He seems to be in kind of a... sticky situation, if you know what I mean.

> **RELL:** *(a little annoyed, but jokingly)* Is he stuck in the bathroom?

> **CLERK:** That may or may not be the case...

> **RELL:** *(going into the office, light)* I'll wait for him inside!

*(Cut to inside: a records office, shelves and document boxes everywhere, mildly chaotic. Rell is alone, looking unhurried despite being on a clock. She looks on the shelves, and in boxes, until she finds a document, she quickly flips through it, and puts it away. She walks towards a desk (not the main one) and puts a thin folder on it, with a feather sticking out. She heads for the door, the director's voice can be heard from behind the bathroom door.)*

> **DIRECTOR PELL:** *(humiliated)* Hey pal, could you bring me some more toilet paper please? And what was that guest's name again?

*(Rell clears her throat)*

> **RELL:** *(voice altered to sound more like the clerk, not answering the second question)* Oh yes mister director, I'll be right back! 

*(She steps out past the clerk, breezy, unreadable.)*

> **RELL:** *(to the clerk, cheerful, while heading for the exit)* Lovely office. Tell the Director I said he's let the place get cluttered. Also, please get him a few more rolls of toilet paper!

*(Hask enters from a vent on the side, bringing some kind of rope after him. He moves through with total efficiency, no wasted motion, loading crystals into bags.)*

*(Once all the crystals are in bags, he pulls out a small charge device from his pack and methodically drills a small hole into the housing support near the building's foundation, the same housing structure Wren repaired earlier. ## Scene 14 - Rell's explanation
*(Thread A & C. ~65 seconds.)*

**SETTING:** Both ships now flying steady, side by side, some distance from the outpost, close enough to still see smoke rising behind them, but far enough to feel safe. Rell has come to the rail of her ship, facing Wren and June across the gap.

> **WREN:** *(sharp, shaken)* What happened back there?

> **RELL:** *(controlled, more serious tone)* Crystal housing failure. The new half.. it's been failing for longer than they've let on. *(pause)* I was in upper management talking about it with the Director himself. Crystal's been failing for weeks now.

> **JUNE:** *(still shaken)* You knew this could happen?

> **RELL:** *(carefully)* I knew it was a risk. Outposts like that, expanded too fast, crystals under more strain than they're built for, it's not exactly rare news. *(quieter)* I am glad you two made it. Truly.

> **WREN:** *(processing, still suspicious)* ...The support line. The one you gave me to install.

> **RELL:** *(back to her usual tone)* Wouldn't have made a difference either way, love. That housing was past holding regardless of what line was on it. *(pause)* You did good work. It just wasn't enough to fix what's actually wrong with that place. Nothing short of a new crystal could have fixed it.

*(Wren doesn't answer right away. June puts a hand briefly on her shoulder.)*

> **JUNE:** *(quieter, trying to steady the moment)* ...Nobody got hurt, right?

> **RELL:** *(a bit too smooth, not really caring)* Storage building. Mostly empty by midday, this time of year. *(turning, calling back toward the helm)* Hask! Set us back on course, we've lingered long enough.

*(She walks off without waiting for further questions. Wren watches her go. Cut.)*
