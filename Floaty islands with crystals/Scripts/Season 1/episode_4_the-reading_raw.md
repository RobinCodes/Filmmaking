## Exposition (read this if something doesn't make sense, otherwise skip until the end):

> This episode picks up shortly after Episode 3, Vey has the planted document and the feather Rell left in the records office.

> The feather is Rell's signature, something that tells Vey it's her. Vey hasn't seen one in a long time. She isn't fully certain it's really Rell, because it's been long enough that anyone could know to leave a feather as a copy or a taunt.

> Rell's exact former position is not revealed this episode, only that she held real rank inside the Spire's crystal operations, enough that she understood housing failures better than current outpost staff, and enough that older clerks still remember her name on sight (see Ep3, Scene 6).

> Why Vey wants to handle this herself (rather than hand it to Brask or Spire security) is not explained yet. The episode should make it clear that she  wants Rell found and wants her unharmed, without saying why.

> Vey's drinking is new,  a direct response to the weight of the crystal crisis, and to being the only person who has the full picture and no one to share it with. She has no family. This should read as private and controlled, not as a public spectacle, something only the audience (and maybe one other small, quiet observer) sees clearly.

### New exposition this episode - the Spire's government:

> The Spire's ruling Council has **5 permanent seats**, each governing one domain:
> - **Castellan** - keeper/administrator of the Spire fortress-island itself: grounds, security, upkeep of the literal seat of government.
> - **Crystal Warden** - head of crystal affairs: growing, distributing, and rationing crystals.
> - **Treasurer** - head of finance.
> - **Commander** - head of law enforcement and the military.
> - **Chancellor** - head of foreign and cluster relations: petitions, outposts, diplomacy with other clusters.

> **Overseer** is not a seat. It's a head-of-state rank, earned or appointed separately from any seat, and can in principle land on any seat-holder. Whoever holds it keeps their seat duties as well. Right now, the rank sits with the Castellan, making Vey "Overseer Castellan Vey." This is why her authority over crystal matters is not automatic from her own seat (Castellan governs the fortress, not crystals), but it's authority she exercises as Overseer, on top of a Crystal Warden seat she doesn't personally hold.

> **Brask is Commander.** 

> **Ottlin is Treasurer.** His cowardice isn't only a personality trait, it's also part of his job. He needs to see numbers before acting on anything.

> **The Crystal Warden and the Chancellor both hold their seats but are unseen this episode** , both away, both summoned back for the coming vote. Their absence is real and complicates the vote's legitimacy, not just background color.

## Scene 1 - The Spire, Mothercrystal Vault, Vey and Brask
*(Thread B. ~40 seconds.)*

**SETTING:** Same vault as Episode 1, Scene 1. Morning. Vey stands before the Mothercrystal, terminal/lectern in front of her. She looks like she hasn't slept well. Brask enters, already holding a report.

> **BRASK:** *(without greeting)* Down again.

> **VEY:** *(flat, already knew)* How much?

> **BRASK:** Three-eighths of a percent. Same as the last four days, give or take.

*(Vey closes her eyes briefly, not dramatic, just a beat of tiredness she allows herself for exactly one second before opening them again.)*

> **VEY:** Four days of identical decline isn't decline anymore, it's a rate.

> **BRASK:** *(carefully, like he's been waiting for her to say this herself)* I know. I've known for a week.

*(Vey looks at him properly for the first time this scene.)*

> **VEY:** (a little bit annoyed) Then why haven't you said "I told you so" yet?

> **BRASK:** *(simply)* Because I'd rather be right with you than without you. *(pause)* That's still on the table, Overseer. For now.

*(He leaves. Vey stays a moment longer, looking at the crystal, then exits the other way. Cut.)*

## Scene 2 - The Spire, lower district dock, Brask and Commander
*(Thread B. ~NEW. ~35 seconds.)*

**SETTING:** A working dock in the Spire's lower district, less ceremonial than the upper Council halls, crates of rationed crystal-powered goods being checked against lists. A small crowd has gathered, tense, a few raised voices. A Guard Captain pushes through to find Brask, who's just arrived from the vault.

> **GUARD CAPTAIN:** *(low, urgent, falling into step beside him)* Commander. Crystal supply is short of the quota again.  Word's already gotten around the dock before we could manage it.

> **BRASK:** *(not slowing)* How short.

> **GUARD CAPTAIN:** Quarter under, maybe more. People are saying it's the third time this week.

*(They round the corner to the crowd. Voices rising:"that's not enough for the whole block," "where's the rest of it," someone shoving a crate off a stack out of frustration rather than aggression. Brask stops, takes it in for a beat before moving.)*

> **BRASK:** *(quiet, to the Captain, not unkind)* It is the third time this week.

*(He steps forward into the crowd. Cut.)*

## Scene 3 - The dock, the unrest (Brask)
*(Thread B. ~NEW. ~55 seconds.)*

**SETTING:** Continuous from Scene 2. Brask moves through the crowd with the calm, practiced authority of someone used to being the most dangerous person in a room without raising his voice. Guards loosely behind him.

> **DOCKWORKER (1):** *(angry, not backing down)* You gonna explain the shortfall, Commander, or just stand there?

> **BRASK:** *(even, direct)* I'm going to explain it. Crystal supply's short. Has been for weeks. Every shipment from here out is going to be lighter than the last one.

*(That lands harder than a denial would have, a ripple through the crowd, a moment of complete slience. Some people get anrgier, some stay quiet.)*

> **DOCKWORKER (2):** *(sharper)* So what, we just go without?

> **BRASK:** *(not flinching)* No. You get told the truth instead of a number that doesn't add up, and you get the Commander standing in front of you instead of a written notice nailed to a post. That's what you get today. *(pause, harder)* What you don't get is to put hands on Spire crates over a quarter shortfall. Next person who does answers to me directly, not to a guard.

*(The crowd doesn't disperse instantly, but the temperature drops. People start moving off, muttering, not satisfied but not escalating either. Brask watches them go, doesn't relax until the dock's mostly clear.)*

> **GUARD CAPTAIN:** *(quietly, once it's over)* That was close, Commander.

> **BRASK:** *(grim, watching the last of the crowd disperse)* It's going to get closer. *(pause)* Get me everything on the next three shipments. I want numbers before the Council does.

*(He turns and walks off. Cut.)*

## Scene 4 - Vey's office, Vey and Reska
*(Thread B. ~75 seconds.)*

**SETTING:** Same office as Episode 3, Scenes 16-17. Vey at her desk. The planted document from the outpost records office sits in front of her, alongside the feather, set carefully to one side like something she keeps meaning to put away and doesn't. Reska enters with a small stack of papers, knocks first.

> **RESKA:** *(quiet, careful)* Overseer. The records you asked for.

> **VEY:** *(not looking up yet)* Set them down.

*(Reska does, but lingers a half-second longer than necessary, her eyes flick to the feather on the desk before she catches herself and looks away.)*

> **VEY:** *(noticing the look, mild)* Something on your mind, Reska?

> **RESKA:** *(too quickly)* No, Overseer.

> **VEY:** *(almost gentle, but pointed)* You're a bad liar. It's one of the better things about you.

*(Reska hesitates, then decides to risk it.)*

> **RESKA:** It's just... the feather reminds me of an old stamp I saw in the old archive boxes. The ones nobody's catalogued properly in years.

*(Vey goes very still.)*

> **VEY:** *(carefully even)* Show me.

> **RESKA:** I don't have it with me, I'd have to go back down and-

> **VEY:** *(standing, already moving, cutting her off)* Then we go now.

*(Cut.)*

## Scene 5 - The Spire, a Council room, Ottlin and Brask
*(Thread B. ~50 seconds.)*

**SETTING:** A modest waiting room outside the main Council chamber, a few chairs, a low table, nothing grand. Ottlin is already seated when Brask enters, mid-conversation as if continuing something from earlier.

> **OTTLIN:**  I'm not saying I disagree, Brask. I'm saying the Overseer hasn't brought it to a vote, and until she does, it's not really a Council matter, is it. It's a... a discussion.

> **BRASK:** *(flatter than usual, the dock still fresh)* It's three structural failures in a week, Ottlin, and a dock full of people who almost put hands on a guard this morning over a quota shortfall. It stops being a discussion at some point.

*(Ottlin notices the change in his tone, this isn't theoretical for Brask anymore.)*

> **OTTLIN:** *(uncomfortable, more careful than before)* ...That said. In principle. If she did bring it to a vote-

> **BRASK:** *(cutting in)* Would you back it?

*(A pause. Ottlin doesn't answer immediately, which is itself an answer.)*

> **OTTLIN:** *(carefully)* I'd want to see the numbers presented properly. In the chamber. Costed, not just counted. *(pause)* That's not me stalling, Brask. That's me doing my job.

> **BRASK:** *(almost amused, not unkind)* That's not a no.

> **OTTLIN:** *(quickly)* It's not a yes, either.

> **BRASK:** *(standing, satisfied enough)* It'll do. For now.

*(He leaves. Ottlin sits a moment, looking slightly like he's said more than he meant to. Cut.)*

## Scene 6 - The old archive, Vey and Reska
*(Thread B. ~65 seconds.)*

**SETTING:** A cramped sub-level archive room, shelves of uncatalogued boxes, dim light. Reska leads Vey to a specific box, pulls it down, opens it. Inside: old requisition slips, sign-out forms, the kind of paperwork nobody thinks to burn.

> **RESKA:** *(pulling out a slip, handing it over)* Keeper sign-ins and sign-outs. Before my time, but they kept everything down here instead of throwing it out. This stamp's on half of them.

*(Vey takes it, looks at a small symbol stamped in the corner, the same shape as the feather, simplified. She doesn't react visibly, but her hand goes still on the paper.)*

> **VEY:** *(quiet)* How many keepers have this mark?

> **RESKA:** *(checking)* Just the one name, far as I can tell. Whoever it was had standing authorization. Senior clearance. *(pause, careful)* Before they stopped, I mean. The sign-outs just... stop, about eleven years back.

> **VEY:** *(almost to herself)* Eleven years.

*(Pause. Reska watches her, unsure whether to ask. She decides not to.)*

> **RESKA:** *(careful)* Should I keep looking, Overseer?

> **VEY:** *(recovering, composed again, "the mask back on")* No. *(pause)* Yes. *(catching the contradiction, almost a dry, tired smile)* Quietly. Just to me. Not to anyone else. Especiall not Brask.

> **RESKA:** *(nodding, understanding more than she lets on)* Understood.

*(Vey takes the box with her as she leaves, cut.)*

## Scene 7 - The Council chamber, meeting
*(Thread B. ~70 seconds. Max on-screen: 3.)*

**SETTING:** A proper chamber, more formal than anywhere we've seen so far, a small horseshoe table for five Council seats, only three occupied, two seats conspicuously empty. Vey enters last. She's composed, public-facing. Brask and Ottlin are already seated.

> **BRASK:** *(as she sits)* Overseer. Glad you could join us.

> **VEY:** *(evenly)* It's my chamber, Brask.

> **BRASK:** *(not backing down, but not hostile either)* Then let's use it. Three structural failures this week. A Mothercrystal output in steady decline for going on two weeks. A dock that nearly turned ugly under my own watch yesterday. I'd like to put the ration cut to a vote.

*(Vey looks at him, then at the two empty seats.)*

> **VEY:** *(carefully)* With two seats absent? You'd call that a legitimate vote, Commander?

> **BRASK:** *(steady)* I'd call it a vote of whoever's willing to show up and do the job. The Crystal Warden's been gone for weeks already. The Chancellor's been avoiding their own office for months. At some point, absence is a vote too, Overseer. It's just a vote for nothing changing.

*(That lands. Ottlin shifts slightly, uncomfortable but not disagreeing.)*

> **VEY:** *(carefully)* On what basi?  Whose numbers?

> **BRASK:** Mine. The outpost reports. Yours too, if you'd let anyone see the readings instead of keeping them to yourself.

*(That lands. Vey doesn't flinch, but something in her jaw tightens.)*

> **VEY:** I keep the readings to myself because panic spreads faster than rationing does, and I have seen what panic costs a cluster firsthand. You haven't.

> **BRASK:** *(quieter, but not backing down)* Then tell me a better plan than waiting, Overseer. Because waiting is what we've been doing, and the crystal doesn't seem to care.

*(Silence. Ottlin looks between them, says nothing yet. Vey takes a long moment.)*

> **VEY:** *(finally, controlled)* Not today. *(pause)* But soon. I have something I need to chase down first. If it's what I think it is, it changes the shape of this conversation entirely.

> **BRASK:** *(skeptical, but willing to wait a bit)* And if it's nothing?

> **VEY:** *(steady)* Then you'll have your vote, and I won't fight you on it.

*(Brask studies her for a long moment, then nods: not satisfied, but willing to hold. He glances at Ottlin.)*

> **BRASK:** I'll hold the vote. 

> **OTTLIN:** *(quiet, mostly to himself)* For what it's worth, I'd like the missing seats back before anyone votes on anything. Numbers feel thinner with empty chairs at the table.

*(Vey nods at that, almost grateful. The session ends without further words. Vhairs shifting, the three of them rising and leaving. Cut.)*

## Scene 10 - Treasurer's office, Ottlin alone
*(Thread B. ~NEW. ~40 seconds.)*

**SETTING:** A new small set: Ottlin's own working space, smaller and more cluttered than Vey's, ledgers stacked with more care than the room itself suggests. Ottlin sits alone, running numbers, muttering to himself.

## Scene 11 - Treasurer's office, lobbied
*(Thread B. ~NEW. ~60 seconds.)*

**SETTING:** Continuous. First Brask enters, direct as ever.

> **BRASK:** *(without preamble)* I need to know if you'll back the rationing vote when it comes.

> **OTTLIN:** *(carefully)* I need to know what it costs before I tell you that.

> **BRASK:** *(impatient, but respecting the ask)* It costs less than another collapse. I can promise you that much in writing if it'll help.

> **OTTLIN:** *(a thin, genuine smile)* It would, actually. Get me real transport and storage figures, not estimates, and you'll have a real answer from me. Not a maybe.

*(Brask nods once, satisfied with that much, and leaves. A short beat later, Vey enters: clearly aware Brask was just here, though she doesn't comment on it.)*

> **VEY:** *(level)* I won't ask what he wanted. I think I already know.

> **OTTLIN:** *(equally level)* Then you already know what I told him.

> **VEY:** *(a small, tired almost-smile)* That you need the numbers.

> **OTTLIN:** *(simply)* Always. From both of you, if it comes to that. *(pause, a little more careful)* I'm not against you, Overseer. I'm not on his side either. I just can't afford to be either of those things before I've seen what it actually costs.

> **VEY:** *(after a moment, genuinely)* That's a more honest answer than most people on this Council give me.

*(She leaves. Ottlin, alone again, looks back down at his ledgers, the weight of being the deciding vote settling on him visibly for the first time. Cut.)*

## Production notes (draft)

- **New builds needed:** lower district dock (Scenes 2-3), Council room (Scene 5), sub-level archive room (Scene 6), Treasurer's office (Scenes 10-11), Council chamber proper (Scene 7), archive-level corridor (Scene 12), Vey's private quarters (Scene 15).
- **Reused sets:** Mothercrystal vault (Scene 1), Vey's office (Scenes 4, 6, 9 and 13), both from previous episodes.
- **Scene 15 is the emotional anchor of the episode**, worth spending extra care on lighting/blocking here even though it's dialogue-light. Consider letting it run a little long; this is the first time the audience sees Vey fully off the clock, and the pacing should feel different from every other scene in the series so far: slower, more held shots, less cutting.
- **Scenes 2-3 (the unrest) are the episode's other big visual beat**, a real crowd, real tension, gives Brask a physically active sequence to contrast against all the Council-room talk. Doesn't need to be huge in scale, but should feel like it could have gone wrong.
- **Reska is a soft introduction**, written deliberately small/minor so she can recur later if useful (either as a quiet ally to Vey, or as a liability if she ever talks). Scene 12 gives her a second, independent function as someone who picks up on Spire gossip/rumor even outside Vey's orbit, useful if we want her to be more than a one-off.
- **The Crystal Warden and Chancellor stay fully unseen this episode**, deliberately, the Crystal Warden's excuse is genuine and almost sympathetic, the Chancellor's is vaguer and seeded with suspicion via Reska overhearing a rumor in Scene 12. Worth deciding now whether the Chancellor's absence becomes a real plot thread (something is actually wrong) or stays a one-off texture beat.
- **What Reska found is intentionally a partial answer**, we learn the mark/feather belongs to one specific former Keeper with senior clearance, and that they stopped eleven years ago. We do NOT learn the name, the reason, or Vey's relationship to them. That's bait for ep5+.
