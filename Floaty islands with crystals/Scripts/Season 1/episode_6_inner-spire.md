# Episode 6: "The Inner Spire"

**Series:** *Floaty Islands with crystals* (working title)
**Runtime target:** 18-24 minutes (measures 11 minutes 35 seconds)
**Threads featured:**

- A (Tethermoor/Wren & June) - primary main storyline, splits from thread C in this episode
- C (The Traders: Rell & Hask) - secondary storyline supporting the main thread. Splits off this episode
- D (Bram/Comic relief) - scattered through the episode, for comedic effect
- B (The Spire) - secondary storyline about the government, until this episode it was mainly eiter at the start or at the end of episodes, but it now begins to scatter trough as we move closer to the threads meeting


---

## Exposition (skip unless something doesn't make sense)

> This episode takes place about a day after Ep5. Rell and Hask split from Wren and June at the Inner Spire gate, heading down toward "the Fallen" (unnamed, unseen this episode) to deliver the crystals stolen in Ep3.

> Chancellor Doran has now physically arrived at the Spire, and he arrived unnervingly fast, well before anyone expected given how far away his office claimed he was. Exactly how fast should stay a little vague in dialogue (nobody states hard travel times), but the wrongness of it should land clearly. This is a seed, not an answer.

> Reska is revealed to answer to Doran's office, not just to record-keeping in general

> Maren (Crystal Warden) is forced to cut her survey short after Bram disables her ship, and heads back toward the Spire early (with partial findings rather than the complete picture she and Vey wanted.) This puts her roughly two days out from the Spire, meaning the Council could plausibly convene while Wren and June are still "below" in Ep8.

> The person of the Overseer is not public knowledge, more trough lack of interest and being seen than the government deliberately keeping it secret.

## Returning Characters

### Thread A - Tethermoor / Wren & June

- **Wren** - protagonist. **Female.** Late teens. The cluster's engineer/fixer - the one who keeps things running when no one else can. Practical, a little stubborn, takes responsibility for things that aren't her fault. Voice should be grounded and direct, she doesn't waste words, doesn't perform emotion, but it leaks through at the edges. Not loud. Most comfortable when she's solving something with her hands.

- **June** - Wren's best friend; airship mechanic and occasional pilot. **Female.**  Confident, a little reckless, talks fast and teases easily, but genuinely reliable when it counts. The contrast to Wren: where Wren is careful and methodical, June is impulsive and improvises. Voice should be loose, energetic, quick with a joke, but capable of being completely serious in a heartbeat when something actually matters.

### Thread C - The "Traders"

- **Rell** - leader of the trading outfit. **Female.** Mid forties. Morally grey, resourceful, has history with the Spire she doesn't explain. Speaks with a light pirate flavor - "ahoy," "aye," -  without playing it as a full pirate caricature, more like an old habit of speech from wherever she's from. Voice should be smooth, unhurried, conversational even mid-con. She rarely raises her voice, can talk a lot but knows when to listen, which makes people fill silences by oversharing. Knows a lot of information.

- **Hask** - Rell's second-in-command. **Male.** Loyal to Rell specifically, not to the work. Skeptical of outsiders, slow to warm up. Voice should be blunt, economical, dry humor delivered flat. Doesn't smile much, isn't humorless. 

### Thread D - Comic relief / Bram

- **Bram** - **Male.** Older, weathered, has owned and lost more airships than he can remember, and likes to talk about them. Not actually sad, performs sadness for sympathy and free drinks. Voice should be loud, theatrical, given to wild and escalating exaggeration. Comic timing comes from overexaggerating, and making up obviously fake stories. Kind of clumsy at times.

### Thread B - The Spire

- **Overseer Castellan Vey** - head of the ruling Council. **Female.** Composed, tired in a way, she hides well. Voice should be measured and quiet even under pressure - she rarely raises her voice, which makes her authority feel earned rather than forced.

- **Councilor Commander Brask** - head of law enforcement and the military. **Male.** Clipped, impatient, treats hesitation as weakness.

- **Councilor Treasurer Ottlin** - head of finance. **Male.** Cautious, non-committal, the kind of person who waits to see which way the wind blows before speaking, though as Treasurer, that caution is at least partly professional, not just temperament. Voice should be soft, fond of phrases like "in principle" and "that said." Not unintelligent, just conflict-averse. Ottlin avoids conflict to protect himself, but he genuinely does need the numbers before he'll move.

- **Reska** - a young records clerk, junior, works near Vey's office. **Female.** Quiet, observant, professionally invisible in the way junior staff often are. Doesn't speak much, but notices everything. Voice should be small, careful, deferential, she's good at her job specifically because she doesn't make herself noticed.

- **Councilor Crystal Warden Maren** - holds the Crystal Warden seat on the Council. **Female, mid-to-late fourties**, roughly Rell's age. Field-first, impatient with Council politics, more comfortable with a crystal in her hands than a gavel. Voice should be brisk, plainspoken, no patience for nonsense, but not unkind. (appears in thread D, but belongs to thread B)

## New Characters

- **Councilor Chancellor Doran** - holds the Chancellor seaton the Council. **Male, approx. 60.** Composed, unhurried, the tone of someone who has never once had to raise his voice to get compliance. Not sinister-sounding on the surface; the discomfort should come from what he says, not how he says it. (finally appears this episode)

- **Aide** - assistant, written female. **young, mid twenties**. May be a one time character, may appear later. We can also replace a courier/clerk from a previous episode. (also her name means helper)

- **Sela** - (Mentioned all the way back in episode 1) a Spire clerk (name-dropped by Doss as "who you'll have to go through"). written **Female, mid thirties to fourties**

### Side characters / one-offs

- **Caravan Master Fenn** -  functional. Runs a supply caravan headed for the outer trade routes; Bram's thread. (has been reacurring so move him out of one-offs when reformatting the script)

- **Guard (Hall Corridor)** - one-off, functional. Namedrops the Hall of Overseers.

- **Maintenance workek** - one-off, has one line

## Scene 1 - Open sky, approaching the Inner Spire

**SETTING:** Both ships closing in on the Inner Spire: walls, cannons, guard towers, exactly as described since ~Ep2. Bigger and colder-looking than the Outer Spire. The Inner Spire is surrounded by an actual wall, supported by many smaller islands, rather than the rope wall of the outer Spire

> **JUNE:** *(low)* Okay. That's a wall that means it.

> **WREN:** *(watching it)* Cannons weren't a figure of speech, then.

## Scene 2 - The gate
*(Thread A & C. ~20 seconds. continuous from previous scene)*

**SETTING:** A guarded entry point, soldiers rather than clerks. Papers checked fast, no charm required or wanted.

> **GUARD:** *(flat, checking papers)* Purpose.

> **RELL:** *(unusually clipped, no banter)* Passing through.

*(The Guard glances at her a bit too long, not suspicious, just the kind of look someone gives a face they almost recognize. Rell doesn't flinch, doesn't help him place it either. He waves them on.)*

> **JUNE:** *(quiet, to Wren)* She didn't even try the "paperwork weather" line.

> **WREN:** *(watching Rell)* No. She didn't.

## Scene 3 - The split
*(Thread A & C. ~50 seconds.)*

**SETTING:** Just past the gate, ships briefly grounded/docked side by side before going separate ways.

> **RELL:** *(businesslike, not unkind)* This is where we peel off, loves!

> **WREN:** *(surprised)* You're not coming in?

> **RELL:** Got business of our own. Business that doesn't much care for guards and gates the way you two need to.

> **JUNE:** *(wary, old habit)* And the "one job" from the outpost?

> **RELL:** *(Loudly)* Called. Paid. Done. *(pause, smaller, almost as a remark but not to herself)* Done's usually flexible, but this once, I mean it.

*(Wren studies her, this is closer to an actual goodbye than anything Rell's given them.)*

> **WREN:** *(quieter, coming from curiousness rather than caring)* Will we see you again?

> **RELL:** *(easy, but something underneath it)* Sky's smaller than it looks, love. Wouldn't bet against it.

*(Hask steps past her toward their ship, pauses by Wren a moment.)*

> **HASK:** *(low, not quite a smile)* Watch which doors you open in there. Not all of 'em close as easy as they open. *(pause)* That's not a warning about Rell. That's a warning about The Spire.

*(He boards without waiting for a response. Rell gives a two-fingered salute and follows. Cut.)*

## Scene 6 - Bram, outpost, "helping"
*(Thread D. ~40 seconds.)*

**SETTING:** The Ep3 outpost, still scarred, still under repair. Maren crouched by the crystal housing with her instrument, focused. Bram nearby, "assisting", which mostly means being in the way and annoying her.

> **BRAM:** *(holding up a wrench)* This one? Or this one? *(holds up a different, unrelated tool)* Or this one, I feel like this one's a real one-

> **MAREN:** *(not looking up, already tired of him)* Put the wrench down.

> **MAREN:** *(finally looking at him, flat)* Do you know anything about crystal housings?

> **MAREN:** *(closing her eyes, annoyed)* Of course you have.

## Scene 7 - Doran arrives
*(Thread B. ~30 seconds.)*

**SETTING:** A formal Spire corridor, Aide's falling into step as Doran walks through, first time he appears. Unhurried, composed, faintly amused by the fuss around his own timing.

> **AIDE:** *(hurrying alongside, genuinely thrown)* Chancellor, the summons only reached your office a day ago. Your last known post was weeks of travel from here.

> **DORAN:** *(calm)* And yet here I am.

> **AIDE:** That's what I don't understand.

> **DORAN:** *(a thin smile, not answering the actual question)* I've always been efficient when it matters.

> **AIDE:** *(pressing, quieter)* Chancellor, forgive me, but how *did* you get here so fast?

> **DORAN:** *(unbothered, already moving past it)* Wouldn't want anyone thinking I'd changed. Consistency matters in a Council.

*(He continues on. The Aide stops, visibly unsettled, watching him go: that wasn't an answer at all. Cut.)*

## Scene 8 - Petitions hall, Wren and June meet Sela
*(Thread A. ~70 seconds.)*

**SETTING:** The backed-up petitions hall. Long line, tired clerks, no Rell to smooth anything. Wren and June wait, then reach a cramped desk near the front, the desk of Sela, tired, overworked, not unkind.

> **JUNE:** *(surveying the line)* This is going to take a while.

> **WREN:** *(tired, sighs)* Everything here takes a while.

*(They reach the front.)*

> **SELA:** *(flat, exhausted)* Name and cluster?

> **WREN:** Wren Catherine Hill. Tethermoor. *(pause)* Doss sent me. Said to ask for you by name.

*(Sela looks up properly for the first time, a flicker of recognition, maybe fondness. Her tone changes from flat to a softer kinder tone)*

> **SELA:** *(softer)* Doss. Hasn't changed, I take it. Still hates coming here himself.

> **WREN:** *(small smile)* Some things don't change.

> **SELA:** *(back to business, apologetic)* I wish I could move you faster. Truth is, we're short two Council seats' worth of authority right now, Crystal Warden's out on survey, Chancellor's wherever he is. *(as a remark)* Altough I heard he just got back, but its impossible taking how far away he was *(back to regular tone)* So everything that needs a real signature is backed up behind both of them.

> **WREN:** So what happens to my petition?

> **SELA:** *(honest)* It sits. Same as forty others. *(pause, kinder)* But I'll flag it. Doss's name still means something to a few of us who remember him. *(standing)* Wait in the side corridor past the second archway, there's a bench, it's quieter than out here. I'll come find you once I've got it logged properly.

> **WREN:** Thank you!

*(Wren nods, not thrilled, but it's something. Cut.)*

## Scene 10 - Reska's report
*(Thread B. ~60 seconds. This scene should NOT reveal its twist early.)*

**SETTING:** A quiet office, could easily read as any senior official's office at first. Reska enters with a small folder, knocks, waits.

> **RESKA:** *(careful, professional)* Sir. You asked to be kept current.

*(We don't see who she's addressing yet, over the shoulder, or from behind, deliberately withholding.)*

> **VOICE:** *(unhurried)* And?

> **RESKA:** The mark on the old sign-out records. It matches a symbol I found elsewhere. *(pause)* Someone with senior clearance, name redacted or missing from what's left. The sign-outs stop eleven years back. *(pause, pressing on before he can ask)* I also pulled your office's own attendance logs, sir, same as I did the others. The gaps go back further than the last few months your office has been giving out.

*(NOW reveal: Doran, seated, unbothered, turning a page of something else entirely as she talks, the audience should feel a small jolt here, recontextualizing the scene retroactively. He doesn't react visibly to the second half of what she's said.)*

> **DORAN:** *(mild)* Bring me what you found. All of it.

> **RESKA:** *(hesitating, half a second too long)* Of course, sir.

> **DORAN:** *(still not looking up)* Have you mentioned any of this to the Overseer?

*(A pause. She has to answer carefully, because the true answer is layered.)*

> **RESKA:** *(evenly)* No, sir. None of it has left this desk.

*(This is technically true, she hasn't told Vey about Doran's absences. She has said nothing here about the fact Vey already "independently" has the feather and the document from Ep3. Doran doesn't ask further, seemingly satisfied.)*

> **DORAN:** *(finally looking at her properly, unreadable)* Good girl. *(pause)* Quietly, Reska. This stays quiet.

> **RESKA:** *(a small nod)* Understood, sir.

*(She turns to leave. Cut.)*

## Scene 11 - Bram breaks the ship
*(Thread D. ~45 seconds.)*

**SETTING:** The outpost dock. Maren's small official Spire vessel, docked, tools scattered where Bram's been "helping" unsupervised.

> **BRAM:** *(triumphant, holding up a component)* I fixed it!

> **MAREN:** *(arriving, alarmed)* Fixed *what?*

> **BRAM:** *(gesturing at the ship, proud)* Whatever this was for!

*(A hissing sound. Something sparks. The ship visibly settles half an inch, listing.)*

> **MAREN:** *(staring, flat, deadly)* That was the lift regulator.

> **BRAM:** *(still holding the piece, slowly losing confidence)* Is that... an important one?

> **MAREN:** *(closing her eyes for patience)* It is the *only* one.

> **BRAM:** *(offering the piece back like that solves it)* I can put it back?

> **MAREN:** *(taking it from him with the exhausted care of someone defusing something)* Don't. Touch. Anything. Ever. Again.

*(She looks at her now-grounded ship, doing math on how many more days this just cost her.)*

> **MAREN:** *(low, furious)* I have survived cluster-wide crystal failures. I have survived Council budget hearings. I am going to survive you out of pure spite.

> **BRAM:** *(brightly, missing the tone entirely)* That's the spirit!

*(Cut.)*

## Scene 13 - Off the path, the Hall of Overseers
*(Thread A. ~45 seconds.)*

**SETTING:** The side corridor Sela pointed them toward. Wren and June, waiting, drift further than the bench she mentioned, one archway too many, into a more formal, guarded corridor.

> **JUNE:** *(looking around)* This doesn't look like the bench she meant.

> **WREN:** *(also unsure, slowly)* No, it does not.

*(They keep walking, curious rather than alarmed, until a Guard steps into their path.)*

> **GUARD:** *(not hostile, just procedural)* This corridor's restricted. You're not cleared for the Hall of Overseers.

> **WREN:** *(blinking)* The what?

> **GUARD:** *(already redirecting them, bored)* Non-staff wait by the archway. This way.

*(He walks them back, unbothered, clearly used to this exact mistake. Once he's out of earshot:)*

> **JUNE:** *(low)* Hall of *Overseers?* Plural?

> **WREN:** *(thinking)* Maybe he just said it wrong. Hall of Overseer... *things*. Portraits, relics, whatever they keep in there.

> **JUNE:** *(considering it, half-joking, half-not)* Or maybe it's exactly what it sounded like. Position's been around forever, right? Nobody rules that long as one person.

> **WREN:** *(uneasy, not dismissing it)* That's... a theory.

> **JUNE:** *(shrugging)* Just saying. Would explain a lot.

*(Neither of them pushes it further. Cut.)*

## Scene 15 - Maren, cutting the trip short
*(Thread D. ~65 seconds.)*

**SETTING:** The outpost dock. Maren, grounded and out of options, flags down a departing supply ship. Fenn is at the helm, loading last crates.

> **MAREN:** *(businesslike, frustrated)* I need passage toward the Spire. Council business. It's urgent, and it's early, and it is entirely because a man I have known for six hours disabled my ship.

> **FENN:** *(shrugging, barely looking up)* Got room. Long as you don't mind cargo company.

> **MAREN:** *(climbing aboard, exhausted)* At this point I'd share a hold with a crate of angry geese. *(pause, muttering, irritated)* Two more days of surveys, gone. I'll be arriving with half the readings I wanted and none of the confidence.

*(The ship pulls away. Some time into the flight, Maren finally sits, instrument bag in her lap, rubbing her eyes. A familiar voice carries from further along the deck.)*

> **BRAM:** *(offscreen, cheerful, mid-story to some unfortunate crate or crewhand)* - and that's when I said to the beast, "not today, friend, not TODAY-"

*(Maren goes very still. Slowly turns her head.)*

> **MAREN:** *(flat, to Fenn, not even looking at him)* Tell me that's not who I think it is.

> **FENN:** *(not looking up from his crates, resigned)* Talked his way aboard not too long ago. Didn't have the heart to say no twice in one week.

> **MAREN:** *(to herself, quiet, sighing)* I hate my life...

> **MAREN:** *(closing her eyes, to Fenn)* He broke my ship.

> **FENN:** *(mild)* He does that.

> **MAREN:** *(standing, already dreading it)* If he touches anything on this ship, Fenn, I am throwing him overboard myself, Council business or not.

> **FENN:** *(calling after her, cheerful)* Wouldn't be the first time someone's tried!

*(Cut.)*

## Scene 16 - Vey and Doran
*(Thread B. ~65 seconds.)*

**SETTING:** Vey's office. Doran seated across from her, composed, unhurried.

> **VEY:** *(even, but pointed)* You took a day. Your own office swore you were weeks out.

> **DORAN:** *(mild)* And here I am. Early, even.

> **VEY:** *(carefully, not believing him)* That's not early, Doran. That's not possible. Not on any ship the Spire's built.

> **DORAN:** *(unbothered, deflecting smoothly)* I had a fast ship and a good tailwind. I wasn't aware punctuality was now a crime on this Council.

> **VEY:** *(not letting it go, but not pushing further either)* It's not a crime. It's a question. You haven't answered it.

> **DORAN:** *(rising slightly, changing tack)* You've summoned the Crystal Warden with the same urgency, I imagine. Has *she* arrived any faster than a good tailwind should allow?

> **VEY:** *(carefully)* She's found something worth the delay.

> **DORAN:** *(a thin smile)* As have I, in my own way. We're all very busy not being where you'd like us, Overseer. I don't imagine that's personal.

*(Vey studies him a long moment, nothing to point to, but nothing that sits right either.)*

> **VEY:** *(flat)* Council convenes on schedule regardless. Whether you've explained yourself by then or not.

> **DORAN:** *(rising, unbothered)* I wouldn't dream of missing it.

*(He leaves without hurry. Vey watches the door a moment after he's gone, filing the unanswered question away. Cut.)*

> **JUNE:** *(barely registering it)* Did you hear that?

> **WREN:** *(stopping, listening)* ...Maybe.

> **JUNE:** *(quiet, after a pause)* Feels different in here without them, doesn't it.

> **WREN:** *(looking around at the fortress around them)* Yeah. *(pause)* Exposed, almost.

## Scene 18 - Vey, alone
*(Thread B. ~40 seconds.)*

**SETTING:** Vey's office, evening. Same desk as always, the feather, the planted document, still sitting where she left them.

*(She looks at the door Doran walked out of. Then at the feather. Picks it up, turns it once.)*

> **VEY:** *(quiet, to herself)* Eleven years, and now you're back just a few days before *he* finally shows his face. *(pause)* A day, for a distance that should've taken weeks. *(quieter)* What aren't you telling me, Doran?

## Production notes (draft)

- **New builds needed:** the Inner Spire gate (Scenes 1-2), petitions area, Doran's private office (Scene 10), the restricted corridor and Hall of Overseers doorway only, no need to see inside (Scene 13), the descent/below establishing shots (Scene 12, deosn't need to be detailed yet, but will be needed for later episodes), Fenn's supply ship interior/deck (Scene 15, can likely reuse a modified version of an existing airship deck).
- **Reused sets:** Vey's office, the Ep3 outpost, open sky shots.
- **Scene 7's editing is important** The reveal only works if the direction/edit withholds Doran's identity for the first few lines, consider blocking it so Reska is framed alone or from behind initially, with Doran only entering frame on his first line (first where he's written as Doran). Note Reska tells the attendance-gap detail unprompted, before Doran asks about Vey, this should read as her deflecting.
- **Doran's impossibly fast arrival is a deliberate seeded mystery**, not meant to resolve this episode. Kept vague on exact numbers to stay consistent with the "one day since Ep5" timeline, nobody states hard travel times, they just react to how wrong it feels.
- **Scene 15 establishes the ticking clock for Ep7-8**: Maren returns with partial findings roughly two days out, meaning the Council vote can plausibly convene while Wren and June are "below" (worth keeping this math consistent when Ep7/8 get scripted.)
 **Time approximations** were made by reading the script and timing it, may be inaccurate because of my reading speed. I think my reading is faster than the actual times, though I do try to read as if I was speaking and wait out some of the acting. (do cinematography to extend the episode)
